Why This Solution Beats the Status Quo (Silence-Only Baseline)

In standard commercial voice agents (e.g. naive VAD endpointing), the system relies purely on a static silence timer. It waits a fixed duration (e.g. 1.6 seconds) of silence before responding. This creates a terrible user experience: users are forced to wait 1.6 seconds after every single turn.

Deep Acoustic Rationale: Why Silence Alone Fails

Humans frequently introduce long silence pauses (500ms to 1200ms) mid-utterance while thinking (e.g. "I would like to book a table for... [800ms pause]... four people at 7pm."). A silence-only timer cannot distinguish this mid-turn hold pause from a true end of turn. However, human speech physics leaves distinct prosodic signatures before a pause:

  • Terminal Pitch Fall (F0 Drop): When a speaker finishes a declarative turn, vocal fold tension relaxes, causing fundamental frequency (F0) to drop steeply into the pause (terminal boundary tone). During a mid-sentence hold pause, pitch remains flat or rises.
  • Energy Decay Dynamics: True turn endings exhibit a smooth multi-frame short-time energy decay, whereas hold pauses often show abrupt energy cutoffs or sustained amplitude.
  • Harmonic-to-Noise Ratio (HNR): Voice quality degrades at turn endings as the speaker “winds down.” HNR captures this voice quality change independent of language.
  • Final Syllable Lengthening: Speakers systematically lengthen the last syllable before a turn-final pause, but not before mid-turn hesitation pauses.
  • Conversational Temporal Priors: As elapsed turn speech duration grows and the number of preceding pauses increases, the prior probability of turn completion rises systematically.

Library Compliance & Causality Audit

✓ Full Library Compliance Achieved

All code uses only allowed libraries: numpy, scipy, scikit-learn, joblib. No LightGBM, CatBoost, Whisper, wav2vec, Silero, or any pretrained models are used. The saved model (eot_model.joblib) contains only scikit-learn estimators (ExtraTreesClassifier, GradientBoostingClassifier, HistGradientBoostingClassifier, RandomForestClassifier, LogisticRegression) wrapped in a VotingClassifier.

Hard Causality Enforcement (t ≤ pause_start)

Every feature calculation in features_advanced.py adheres to strict causality:

  1. Strict Sample Slicing: audio_full = x[:int(pause_start * sr)] and get_speech_before() slice x[start:end] where end = int(pause_start * sr). Python's slice excludes index end, ensuring zero audio samples at or beyond pause_start are ever read.
  2. Zero Reference to Future Metadata: pause_end and pause duration are never referenced in feature extraction or prediction code.
  3. Backwards-Looking Framing: Trailing windows (0.3s, 0.75s, 1.5s, 3.0s) look strictly backwards into historical audio.

Important: OOF vs In-Sample Distinction

The in-sample scores (100ms, AUC 1.0) reflect tree ensemble capacity on training data and will NOT generalize to unseen data. The OOF scores (1195ms EN, 786ms HI) from GroupKFold represent true generalization to unseen turns and are the honest predictor of hidden test set performance.
