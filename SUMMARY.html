Executive Summary & Results Comparison

In voice AI architectures, traditional endpointing relies on static silence timers (e.g. waiting 1.6 seconds of silence). This introduces heavy conversational latency. Our machine learning solution analyzes multi-resolution prosodic and spectral cues strictly prior to pause onset to predict p_eot (probability of turn end).

Solution Architecture & Causality Compliance

Strict Causality Guarantee

For any pause starting at pause_start, feature extraction enforces x_before = x[0 : int(pause_start * sr)]. No future samples (audio after pause_start) or post-pause metadata (such as pause_end duration) are touched. A live agent cannot hear the future.

Extracted Feature Vector (48 Dimensions)

  • Prosodic & Pitch ($F_0$) Contour: Fundamental frequency estimated via normalized autocorrelation. Features capture pitch trajectory, pitch slope over the final voiced region (falling pitch = statement completion; flat/rising pitch = continuation hold), and relative pitch drop ratio against turn average pitch.
  • Energy Dynamics: Short-Time Energy (dB) contour across trailing windows (300ms, 750ms, 1500ms), energy decay slope into the pause, and trailing energy ratio.
  • Spectral & Timbral Cues: Zero-Crossing Rate (ZCR) dynamics to detect unvoiced fricatives (/s/, /f/, /th/) at word boundaries, Spectral Centroid, Spectral Rolloff (85%), Spectral Flux, and 10 MFCC coefficients + standard deviations.
  • Conversational Temporal Context: Elapsed turn speech time ($t = \text{pause\_start}$) and pause sequence index (0, 1, 2, ...).

Model Ensembling & Calibration

The solution fits a Soft Voting Classifier combining diverse CPU-friendly models:

  • CatBoost Classifier (Weight: 35%) — Excels at capturing non-linear feature interactions and decision boundaries.
  • LightGBM Classifier (Weight: 25%) — Gradient boosted trees providing rapid decision splits.
  • Extra Trees Classifier (Weight: 20%) — Random subspace trees preventing over-fitting.
  • Logistic Regression with Standard Scaling (Weight: 20%) — Linear regularized baseline ensuring smooth probability calibration.

Summary of NOTES.md & Failure Analysis

Where the Model Fails (Root Cause Analysis)

  1. Abrupt Noisy Cutoffs: Short pauses where speech ends abruptly due to ambient background noise or phone line clipping, leaving insufficient trailing voiced frames for pitch slope calculation.
  2. Interrogative Intonation: Questions ending with a high continuation pitch rise, where the turn is syntactically finished but prosodically resembles a hold pause.

What We Would Do With One More Day

  • Implement streaming pitch tracking (YIN/PYIN) with adaptive bandpass noise filtering to extract pitch contours in low SNR telephony environments.
  • Incorporate continuous speech rate estimation (syllable nuclear energy bursts per second) and micro-prosodic energy acceleration deltas.
  • Explore lightweight 1D Depthwise-Separable Convolutions on raw spectrograms combined with multi-task cross-lingual domain adaptation for CPU deployment.
